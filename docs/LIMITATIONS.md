# Limitations and open obligations

- This is not an RH proof.
- The input is the completed `Xi` function, which already encodes its zeros.
- Finite moment/Hankel/S-fraction positivity does not prove positivity at every order.
- The global positive measure `dπ` and infinite self-adjoint Jacobi operator remain conjectural until proved.
- Moment inversion is ill-conditioned and requires high precision.
- GUE evidence currently concerns finite, stability-selected blocks, not global universality.
- Bootstrap resampling of a short spacing list is a robustness diagnostic, not independent data.
- Independent implementations and frozen-rule replications are required before strong novelty claims.
